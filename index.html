Important Information Regarding Aadhaar Seeding for Stipend Disbursement

To receive your stipend, it is mandatory that your Aadhaar is seeded to the bank account (Note that seeding and linking are different processes) where you wish to receive the funds. The objective is to ensure your bank account is Aadhaar-linked and mapped with NPCI for Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). This step is crucial for smooth and timely stipend credit.

The process involves visiting your bank and explaining the purpose â€” Aadhaar seeding for stipend (DBT) under a government-funded programme. Unfortunately, not all bank staff are familiar with this specific requirement, so be prepared to explain it clearly.

Here's what you need to take:

  • Two filled Aadhaar seeding forms
  • Photocopy of your Aadhaar card
  • Any other documents the bank may request

Submit one form along with the required documents. The second form should be used to get a stamp and signature from the bank official as proof of submission, along with the IIN. Politely ask for the IIN number (Issuer's Identification Number) of your bank branch â€” though note that many officials may not know this. If required, escalate the request to a higher authority in the branch.

If the bank fails to provide this, or you wish to check your bankâ€™s status yourself, refer to the official NPCI list of supported banks:
https://www.npci.org.in/what-we-do/nach/live-members/live-banks

After submission, you can check whether your Aadhaar has been successfully seeded and mapped in two ways:

  • UIDAI website (after logging in with Aadhaar)
  • NPCIâ€™s Aadhaar Mapper Status

Take the signed and stamped form with you on the document verification and admission date.
